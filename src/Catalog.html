SPACE SUITS


The SpaceX space suits are designed to be functional, lightweight, and to offer protection from potential depressurization. The suits are custom made for each crew member to ensure a perfect fit.

IVM and EVM Activity Space Suits


IVM (IntraVehicular Mobility)


IVM suits are designed for activities inside spacecraft or space stations. Their primary uses include:

Thermal protection: Maintaining a stable temperature in environments with variable conditions.
Mobility: Allowing astronauts to move freely while performing tasks.
Life support: Providing oxygen and removing carbon dioxide during operations inside the vehicle.


EVM (ExtraVehicular Mobility)


EVM suits, also known as EVA (Extravehicular Activity) suits, are meant for activities outside the spacecraft, such as spacewalks. Key uses include:

Environmental protection: Protecting astronauts from the harsh conditions of space, including extreme temperatures and micrometeoroids.
Life support: Offering a self-contained life support system for breathing and temperature control during spacewalks.
Mobility and tools: Enabling movement and the use of tools in a zero-gravity environment.


Both types of suits are crucial for ensuring astronaut safety and facilitating various operations in the unique environments of space.


STARLINK

Starlink is a satellite internet constellation being constructed by SpaceX to provide satellite
internet access globally. The project aims to offer high-speed broadband internet,
particularly to underserved and remote areas where traditional internet services are limited or unavailable.

HOW IT WORKS


Starlink works by deploying thousands of small satellites in low Earth orbit (LEO).

These satellites communicate with ground stations and user terminals (dish antennas) to deliver internet service. The deployment of satellites at low altitude reduces latency compared to traditional satellite internet systems.


KEY FEATURES


Speed: Starlink typically offers download speeds ranging from 50 Mbps to 150 Mbps, with latency around 20 to 40 milliseconds.
Coverage: It aims to provide internet service worldwide, with initial focus on rural and remote regions.
User Equipment: Users require a Starlink Kit that includes a satellite dish (also known as a phased-array antenna) and a Wi-Fi router.

LAUNCH AND DEPLOYMENT


Starlink began launching satellites in 2019, and as of early 2025, thousands of satellites have been deployed.
The constellation is expected to expand significantly in the coming years.

CURRENT STATUS


Starlink has been in beta testing since 2020, with users able to sign up for the service.
It has seen increased adoption worldwide, with various government programs and partnerships to expand access.

CHALLANGES


There are concerns regarding space debris, the impact on astronomical observations, and regulatory hurdles in various countries.

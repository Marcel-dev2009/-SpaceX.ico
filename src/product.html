FALCON --9


The Falcon 9 is a reusable rocket by SpaceX, designed for
launching satellites and crew missions. It features:

Two stages: First stage for lift-off, second for payload delivery.

Reusability: The first stage lands back for refurbishment

Payload capacity: Up to 22,800 kg to low Earth orbit.

Proven track record: Over 200 successful missions since 2010.


DRAGON


DRAGON


The SpaceX Dragon is a reusable spacecraft for transporting cargo and crew to the International Space Station (ISS).
It includes the Cargo Dragon for supplies and Crew Dragon for astronauts. Notably,
Crew Dragon was the first commercial spacecraft to carry astronauts to the ISS in May 2020.
Both variants are launched on the Falcon 9 rocket and designed for reusability to lower mission costs.

STARSHIP


The SpaceX Starship is a fully reusable spacecraft designed for transporting humans and cargo to destinations
like the Moon, Mars, and beyond. It features a two-stage system: the Starship upper stage and the
Super Heavy booster. Starship aims to enable interplanetary travel and is central to SpaceX's long-term vision of colonizing Mars.
Its first orbital test flight occurred in April 2023, showcasing its potential for future space missions.
